# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary audience:** Tourists visiting Porto seeking a memorable, credible wine experience + local residents and food/wine enthusiasts looking for education and community + corporate groups booking tastings.

**Context:** Visitors arrive curious but maybe unsure if this is for them. Locals return for learning and the sommelier's expertise. Corporate groups want prestige + professionalism.

**Job to be done:** Discover a wine experience worth your time and money. Understand what makes Dogma different (the Master of Port). Book a tasting that fits your level and interest.

## Product Purpose

Dogma Wine Bar is Porto's premium wine destination led by Portugal's first Master of Port Wine. The site communicates credibility, accessibility, and the human passion behind the bar — so visitors (tourists, locals, groups) trust Dogma enough to book a tasting and return.

Success: every visitor leaves understanding *who* Vasilii is, *why* he matters (Master of Port credential), and *how to book* a tasting (one click away, always).

## Brand Personality

**Voice:** Acolhedor, educador, apaixonado — but grounded in expertise, not amateurish.

Tone: invite guests in warmly; teach without condescension; show genuine love for wine and terroir. Confident in the credential (Master of Port), but never gatekeeping. Sound like a sommelier who wants you to enjoy wine, not a professor who judges you for not knowing enough.

Emotions to evoke: confidence (this person knows), curiosity (I want to taste), belonging (I'm welcome here), discovery (there's something special happening).

## Anti-references

**Stuffy/elitist:** Don't be the wine snob gatekeeping experience. No "only true connoisseurs" messaging. No inaccessible jargon without explanation.

**Generic AI-default (cream/sand + cards + eyebrows everywhere):** The palette and structure are fine (deep burgundy + cream is on-brand), but reject the *reflex*. Every section needn't have a gold eyebrow; spacing and hierarchy should vary. No "infinite cards" pattern just because it's safe.

**Kitsch or over-decorated:** Don't lean into "wine is elegant so let's add fancy ornaments." Oriental arches, stars, seals are *intentional* credentials (Star Wine List, Master of Port seal), not just decoration. No gratuitous flourishes.

## Design Principles

1. **Credential first.** Master of Port is the spine. It appears in the hero, the central section, the sommelier quote, the core copy. Visitors should see "I'm led by the person with the highest Port credential in Portugal" within the first screen.

2. **Nothing fake, everything real or intentional.** No grey-box placeholders. If a photo isn't shot yet, design an intentional, beautiful empty state (like the VG crest instead of "Vasilii's Portrait"). Every link goes somewhere; every call-to-action books a real experience.

3. **Accessible premium.** Deep burgundy + warm cream + restrained gold is upmarket without being cold. WCAG AA contrast everywhere. Invite guests in; don't make them prove they belong.

4. **One clear path to taste.** Booking a tasting is the primary conversion. It's in the hero, the services, the final CTA, and the mobile header (always reachable). Every other section builds the case for why to book.

5. **Show craft, not AI.** Thoughtful spacing, restrained motion, considered typography. Reject the reflex (eyebrows, cards, gradients just because). Every design choice is because it serves credibility or clarity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

**WCAG AA.** All body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Large text ≥3:1. Navigation keyboard-accessible. Forms have proper labels. Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color contrast on links and interactive elements meets AA.

**Inclusive tone:** Wine language is jargon-heavy; the site explains gently (terroir, tawny, vintage) without being pedantic. Multiple entry points for different skill levels (Portuguese Wine Tasting vs. Port Wine Master Class).

**Considerations:** Reduced motion (animations are subtle, not default). Reduced cognitive load on mobile (one column, clear priority). Visibility for colorblind visitors (don't rely on color alone to convey information).
